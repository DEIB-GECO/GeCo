Inspired by our work on genomic data integration, during the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic we searched for effective ways to help mitigate its effects with our contribution; we were able to successfully re-apply the data-driven paradigm used for human genomics.
Even if the domain of viral genomics is completely new, it presents many analogies with our previous challenges. Here we model viral nucleotide sequences as strings of letters, with corresponding sub-sequences -- the genes -- that encode for amino acid proteins.
Aspects related to the technology, biology, and organization of viral sequencing are also reported. The model has set the basis for a rich stream of research that applies data-driven methods to practically support virology research. Within a large data warehouse, we integrate sequences with their metadata from a variety of different sources; we make it searchable through the ViruSurf (http://www.gmql.eu/virusurf/) search interface, allowing to extract sequences based on their combined mutations, to compare different conditions, and to build interesting populations for downstream analysis. To such search system, we associate a powerful data visualization application called VirusViz (http://www.gmql.eu/virusviz/), which receives the results of queries and provides metadata summarization, variant descriptions, and variant visualization. VirusViz offers a rich customization and the possibility of grouping and comparatively analyzing populations of interest (allowing, for instance, to trace new variants since their starting dates in a precise location). Several other systems are currently under development.
