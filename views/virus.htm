Inspired by our work on genomic data integration, after the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic we turned our attention to the analysis and management of viral sequences, aiming at re-applying the data-driven paradigm used for human genomics.

The GeCo approach to the domain of viral genomics presents many analogies with our previous approach to human genomics.

We observed the need for data integration across heterogeneous sources collecting viral sequences, we thus modeled a consensus conceptual schema for both metadata and sequence properties (mutations of viral sequences resulting into changes of the amino-acids forming proteins) and we built search systems over sequence repositories, with powerful data visualization tools.

Results include:
  • the Viral Conceptual Model (VCM); describing sequences with their annotations, nucleotide mutations and amino acid changes; aspects related to the technology, biology, and organization of viral sequencing are also reported. The model has set the basis for a rich stream of research that applies data-driven methods to practically support virology research.
  • Within a large data warehouse, we integrate sequences with their metadata from a variety of different sources; we make it searchable through the ViruSurf (http://www.gmql.eu/virusurf/) search interface, supporting the extraction of sequences based on their combined mutations, to compare different conditions, and to build interesting populations for downstream analysis.
  • To such search system, we associate a powerful data visualization application called VirusViz (http://www.gmql.eu/virusviz/), which receives the results of queries and provides metadata summarization, variant descriptions, and variant visualization. VirusViz offers a rich customization and the possibility of grouping and comparatively analyzing populations of interest (allowing, for instance, to trace new variants since their starting dates in a precise location).
  • EpiSurf (http://www.gmql.eu/episurf/) is a search engine for selecting sequences and epitopes and integrating them for testing epitope stability.
  • ViruClust (http://www.gmql.eu/viruclust/ and https://github.com/DEIB-GECO/Docker_ViruClust/) is tool for fine tuning of clusters of sequences, used for fine-grain surveillance.
  • VariantHunter (http://gmql.eu/variant_hunter/) is an application that monitors the evolution of mutations and indicates possible emerging variants.
  • CoV2K is an abstract model integrating data and knowledge aspects of SARS-CoV-2 (see API documentation at http://gmql.eu/cov2k/api/).
Other systems are currently under development.
